The Story of Sunbula

In the fertile valleys of Jenin, where golden wheat sways with the breeze and the land whispers stories of generations past, the seed of Sunbula was planted. Legend tells of a wise village woman who sat beneath an old olive tree, stitching her dress with care. When a curious child asked what she was making, she replied, "I’m not just sewing fabric — I’m stitching memory, preserving a story, and shaping the future." These words became the spark of inspiration. A group of women, bound by love for their heritage and a desire for change, began gathering — not in studios or workshops, but in kitchens and courtyards. With every embroidered thread and hand-felted shape, they poured in their stories, resilience, and dreams. What began as a humble gathering blossomed into Sunbula — a platform not only for handcrafted art but for the voices behind it. Today, Sunbula celebrates Palestinian tradition, empowers women, and connects timeless crafts with the world.
